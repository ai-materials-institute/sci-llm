# Highlighted Properties

**Paper:** Production and characterization of surfactin-like biosurfactant produced by novel strain *Bacillus nealsonii* S2MT and it's potential for oil contaminated soil remediation

**Authors:** Phulpoto et al. (2020), *Microbial Cell Factories* 19:145

---

## Surface Activity

- **Surface tension (SFT):** 34.15 ± 0.6 mN/m (reduced from 81.3 mN/m for distilled water)
- **Measurement method:** Du Nouy ring method (digital tensiometer JYW-200B), distilled water used as standard, evaluations in triplicates

## Emulsification

- **Emulsification index (EI₂₄):** 55 ± 0.3% for kerosene oil after 24 hours
- **Measurement method:** Cell-free supernatants (2 mL) mixed with equal amount of kerosene on vortex mixer for 2 min, left undisturbed at room temperature for 24 h; EI = (height of emulsified layer after 24 h / total height of liquid) × 100

## Critical Micelle Concentration (CMC)

- **CMC value:** 40 mg/L
- **SFT at CMC:** 34.5 ± 0.56 mN/m (reduced from 81.2 mN/m), with no significant change in SFT beyond this concentration
- **Measurement method:** Different concentrations (0–100 mg/L) of biosurfactant solution prepared and surface tension measured

## Biosurfactant Characterization

- **Type:** Cyclic lipopeptides relating to surfactin-like isoforms (C¹³–C¹⁵)
- **Identification methods:** Thin-layer chromatography (TLC), ultra high performance liquid chromatography and mass spectrometry (UHPLC-MS)
